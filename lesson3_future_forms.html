Grammar: will vs going to

10 min
Elicit before presenting. Ask: "How do you say 'yarın gidiyorum' in English?" — let them try first. Then present the forms. The KEY question to drill: "Did you decide this before or just now?"
WILL
Spontaneous decision
"The phone's ringing." → "I'll get it!" (you decide NOW)
WILL
Prediction (opinion/belief)
"I think it will rain tomorrow." (no evidence, just belief)
WILL
Offer / Promise
"I'll help you with that." / "I'll call you later."
GOING TO
Plan / Intention (already decided)
"I'm going to visit my parents." (you decided BEFORE)
GOING TO
Prediction with evidence
"Look at those clouds — it's going to rain!" (you can SEE)
GOING TO
Strong intention
"She's going to start university in September." (arranged)
FormPositiveNegativeQuestion
willI will travel / I'll travelI won't travel / I will not travelWill you travel?
going toI am going to travel / I'm going to travelI'm not going to / She isn't going to travelAre you going to travel?
CCQ drill: (1) "I think it will rain" — is this planned? (2) "I'm going to call my mum — I told her yesterday" — planned or spontaneous? (3) "I'll have the pasta!" — why will? Get all 4 students to answer one CCQ before moving to exercises.

Grammar Reference — will vs going to

WILL
  • ✅ Predictions: "I think it will rain."
  • ✅ Spontaneous: "I'll answer the phone!"
  • ✅ Offers: "I'll help you."
GOING TO
  • ✅ Plans: "I'm going to visit her."
  • ✅ Evidence: "Look — it's going to rain!"
  • ✅ Already decided: "She's going to study law."
