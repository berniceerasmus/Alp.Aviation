Lesson Objectives

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to...
Language Goals

Communicative Goals
  • Describe a photo or situation in the present moment

Lesson Timeline

45 min total
0:00 – 0:08 | 8 min
📋 Warm-Up: Homework Review + Prediction Discussion
Each student reads 1 prediction from their L3 homework. Group reacts: agree or disagree? Use connector chain: "I agree — I also think... will... because..." This activates L1 connectors, L2 past tense, AND L3 future forms simultaneously. Powerful warm-up.

📖 Input: Present Continuous
Point to something happening NOW in the room — someone is sitting, the light is on, traffic is moving outside. Elicit: "What is he doing?" → Build the form. Present the grammar table. Key question to drill: "Is this a habit or is it happening RIGHT NOW?"
0:16 – 0:30 | 14 min
✏️ Exercises 1, 2 & 3 — Controlled Practice
MC (6 min): individual then pairs. Fill-in (4 min): individual check. Tense sort activity (4 min): students categorise sentences by tense. Keep pace brisk — these students now have the foundation, this is consolidation.
0:30 – 0:38 | 8 min
🔗 Exercise 4 & 5 — Mixed Tenses + Question Formation
Mixed tense paragraph completion (5 min) — this is the hardest exercise. Do it collaboratively if needed. Question formation (3 min) — write the question for the given answer. This tests tense recognition from the other direction.
0:38 – 0:45 | 7 min
🗣️ Speaking: Photo Description + Group Reflection
Photo description (2 min) using Present Continuous. Then: each student tells the group one thing they CAN do now that they couldn't do in Lesson 1. End with applause. This is how you build lasting motivation.

Grammar: Present Continuous

8 min
Don't start with the table. Point at something in the room and ask "What is happening right now?" Let them try. Then present the form. The PS vs PC contrast is the most important thing — drill it with CCQs.
FormPositiveNegativeQuestion
Present ContinuousI am working / I'm working
She is working / She's working		I'm not working
She isn't working		Are you working?
Is she working?
Present Continuous — use for:
✅ RIGHT NOW: "She is talking on the phone."
✅ TEMPORARY: "I am working in Istanbul this month."
✅ CHANGING SITUATION: "English is becoming more important."
✅ FUTURE ARRANGEMENT: "I am meeting her at 6."
Present Simple — use for:
✅ HABITS: "She talks on the phone every day."
✅ FACTS: "Water boils at 100°C."
✅ ROUTINES: "I work in Istanbul."
✅ PERMANENT: "English is important."
CCQ drill: (1) "He works in a bank" — happening now or habit? (2) "He is working late tonight" — temporary or permanent? (3) "I'm studying English" — can I say "I study English right now"? Why not?

All Four Tenses — Reference

for reference
Use this as a visual reference during exercises. Don't teach this — by Lesson 4, students should recognise these forms. Ask THEM to fill in the examples.
Present Simple
I work / She works
Habits, facts, routines
"I usually wake up at 7am."
Past Simple
I worked / She went
Finished past actions
"She studied English last year."
Future (will / going to)
I will go / I'm going to go
Predictions, plans, offers
"I'm going to travel next month."
Present Continuous
I am working / She is going
Now, temporary, arrangements
"He is talking on the phone."
