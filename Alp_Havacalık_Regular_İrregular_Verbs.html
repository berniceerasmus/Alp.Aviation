200 Regular & Irregular Verbs

A2–B1 study list for speaking, work, travel, problems, meetings, daily life, and technical situations.

Alp Havacılık English Group

How to Study This List

1. Learn Meaning

Start with the Turkish meaning and example sentence.

2. Learn Past Simple

This is the most important form for your next test.

3. Say It Aloud

Use the pronunciation help and repeat the example sentence.

4. Make Your Own Sentence

Write one simple present and one simple past sentence.

Important: For regular verbs, pronounce -ed in three ways: /t/ walked, booked, missed; /d/ cleaned, played, called; /id/ wanted, needed, started.

Memory Patterns

No Change

put-put-put, cut-cut-cut, hurt-hurt-hurt

Vowel Change

drink-drank-drunk, speak-spoke-spoken, write-wrote-written

ought / aught

buy-bought-bought, teach-taught-taught, catch-caught-caught

Regular -ed

work-worked-worked, visit-visited-visited, prepare-prepared-prepared

Pronunciation Drills

/t/ ending

missed, booked, worked, fixed, stopped

/d/ ending

played, called, learned, cleaned, saved

/id/ ending

wanted, needed, visited, started, waited

Difficult Irregulars

thought, bought, brought, taught, caught, wrote, flew, knew
